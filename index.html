Collective Behavior Model

My current research develops models for swarm robotics. A recent contribution is a minimal collective behavior model that captures emergent patterns such as flocking, swarming, and vortexing (for small populations). Unlike classical Vicsek, Cucker–Smale, or Olfati–Saber models, it achieves collision-free, spatially flexible dynamics through two tunable parameters — the spatial offset and kinetic offset — enabling smooth transitions between ordered flocking and exploratory swarming.

The model considers a robot collective \( \mathcal{N} = \{1, \ldots, n\} \) with positions \( \mathbf{p}_i \in \mathbb{R}^d \) and velocities \( \mathbf{v}_i \in \mathbb{R}^d \), governed by

\[ \dot{\mathbf{p}}_i = \mathbf{v}_i, \quad \dot{\mathbf{v}}_i = \mathbf{u}_i, \]

where \( \mathbf{u}_i = \mathbf{u}_{i,\mathrm{agg}} + \mathbf{u}_{i,\mathrm{ali}} \) combines aggregation and alignment interactions within a neighborhood \( \mathcal{N}_i \).

  • Aggregation: \( \mathbf{u}_{i,\mathrm{agg}} = \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i} \psi(\lVert \mathbf{p}_{ij} \rVert)\mathbf{p}_{ij} \), with \( \psi(\lVert \mathbf{p}_{ij} \rVert) = 1 - \left(\frac{\delta_i |\mathcal{N}_i|}{\lVert \mathbf{p}_{ij} \rVert}\right)^{\alpha_i} \)
  • Alignment: \( \mathbf{u}_{i,\mathrm{ali}} = \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i} \phi(\lVert \mathbf{v}_{ij} \rVert)\mathbf{v}_{ij} \), with \( \phi(\lVert \mathbf{v}_{ij} \rVert) = 1 - \left(\frac{\eta_i}{|\mathcal{N}_i| \lVert \mathbf{v}_{ij} \rVert}\right)^{\beta_i} \)
  • Spatial offset \( \delta_i \) defines equilibrium spacing; kinetic offset \( \eta_i \) regulates coordination intensity
  • Interaction functions are bounded via tanh, ensuring smooth cooperative or repulsive responses

Flocking Model

This model formalizes the alignment mechanism in collective motion, where each agent adjusts its velocity to match that of its neighbors. The alignment acceleration for agent \( i \) is defined as:

\[ \mathbf{a}_{i,\mathrm{ali}} = \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i} \phi(\lVert \mathbf{v}_j - \mathbf{v}_i \rVert) (\mathbf{v}_j - \mathbf{v}_i), \quad \phi(\lVert \mathbf{v}_j - \mathbf{v}_i \rVert) = 1 - \left( \frac{\eta_i} {|\mathcal{N}_i| \lVert \mathbf{v}_j - \mathbf{v}_i \rVert} \right), \]

where \( \eta_i > 0 \) denotes the kinetic offset controlling sensitivity to relative velocity differences, and \( |\mathcal{N}_i| \) is the number of neighbors within the interaction zone.

In the simplified form, the interaction potential can be expressed as:

\[ \mathbf{a}_{i,\mathrm{ali}} = \sum_{j \in \mathcal{N}_i} \phi (\mathbf{v}_j - \mathbf{v}_i), \quad \phi = \frac{1}{|\mathcal{N}_i|}. \]

  • Encourages local velocity synchronization among agents
  • Ensures smooth collective motion through distributed averaging
  • Scales naturally with neighborhood size \( |\mathcal{N}_i| \)
