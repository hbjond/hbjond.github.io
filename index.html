Research

My group explores mathematical models and control algorithms for teams of aerial robots. We combine rigorous analysis, optimal control, and simulation with experimental validation on quadrotor teams. Key themes:

  • Bearing-distance based flocking and collision-avoidance strategies
  • Integral sliding mode control combined with metaheuristics for adaptive tuning
  • Differential-flatness based trajectory generation for multi-quadrotor coordination
  • Perception-limited local interaction laws and platooning

Selected highlights

  • Designed robust formation controllers validated in simulation for 10+ agents
  • Published work on bearing-distance flocking and zone-based interactions
  • Open-source MATLAB and Python code for boid models and trajectory generation
